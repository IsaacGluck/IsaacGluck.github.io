About

Hi, my name is Isaac Gluck. I am an aspiring software engineer at Dartmouth College. I began my coding career at Stuyvesant High School and have not looked back. I've taken classes in Python, Java, and software development (HTML, CSS, and Javascript). Outside the classroom I've delved further into web development, learning PHP, SQL, Mongo, and jQuery on my own time. I'm currently learning Node.js and React.

Beyond the screen, I am an avid soccer fan and player (you'll never walk alone) and have just fallen in love with ultimate frisbee. I have played the violin my entire life and recently taught myself guitar.

Darby Doomsday Website
The Darby Doomsday website is a partner website to the Darby Doomsday book series written by Professor Ernest Hebert. The website contains sneak peeks to the books, a gallery of artwork pertaining to the series, an interactive map, and sections where the author can share blog posts, essays, and poems.

I helped to develop the website with a team from the Digital Arts, Leadership, & Innovation Lab. I worked on the gallery section, the interactive map, and mobile compatibility.
